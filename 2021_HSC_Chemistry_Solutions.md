### Question 32 (4 marks)

**Analysis of neutralization enthalpies:**

**Reactions 1 and 2 (ŒîH = -57.6 kJ mol‚Åª¬π):**
- HCl + KOH ‚Üí KCl + H‚ÇÇO
- HNO‚ÇÉ + KOH ‚Üí KNO‚ÇÉ + H‚ÇÇO

**Reaction 3 (ŒîH = -12.0 kJ mol‚Åª¬π):**
- HCN + KOH ‚Üí KCN + H‚ÇÇO

**Explanation:**

**Reactions 1 and 2 have the same enthalpy because:**
- Both HCl and HNO‚ÇÉ are strong acids that completely ionize in solution
- KOH is a strong base that completely ionizes
- The actual neutralization reaction in both cases is: H‚Å∫(aq) + OH‚Åª(aq) ‚Üí H‚ÇÇO(l)
- The enthalpy change represents the energy released when H‚Å∫ and OH‚Åª ions combine to form water
- This is independent of the spectator ions (Cl‚Åª, NO‚ÇÉ‚Åª, K‚Å∫)

**Reaction 3 has a different enthalpy because:**
- HCN is a weak acid that only partially ionizes in solution
- Energy is required to ionize HCN: HCN(aq) ‚Üí H‚Å∫(aq) + CN‚Åª(aq)
- The overall process involves both ionization of HCN and neutralization
- The net enthalpy is less negative because energy is consumed in the ionization step
- ŒîH(overall) = ŒîH(ionization) + ŒîH(neutralization)
- Since ionization is endothermic, the overall process releases less energy
